#### 3.1 Ability Scores (Left Column – The Six Core Stats)

These are the **foundation** of your entire character. Every action that involves skill, strength, smarts, willpower, or social presence uses one of these six numbers.

You enter a number between roughly 3 and 20 (higher is possible with magic items later).  
For each score, the sheet instantly shows a **modifier** in big gold text below it (example: 16 → **+3**).  
The modifier is what actually gets added (or subtracted) to dice rolls.  
**Quick formula the sheet uses** (you don’t need to remember it):  
Modifier = (Ability Score − 10) ÷ 2, rounded down.

**What each ability actually means in the game** (official 5e explanations simplified for beginners):

- **Strength** (STR)  
  Raw physical power — how much you can lift, how hard you hit with heavy weapons (axes, greatswords, mauls), how good you are at wrestling/grappling/shoving enemies, breaking doors, bending bars, wearing heavy armor without slowing down.  
  **Most important for**: Barbarians, Fighters (melee), Paladins who want to hit hard.  
  **Common checks**: Athletics (climbing, jumping, swimming), breaking things, intimidating someone with raw presence.

- **Dexterity** (DEX)  
  Agility, reflexes, hand-eye coordination, balance — how nimble and quick you are.  
  Affects ranged weapon attacks (bows, thrown daggers), finesse melee weapons (rapiers, daggers), Armor Class (how hard you are to hit), Initiative (who goes first in combat), and sneaking/hiding.  
  **Most important for**: Rogues, Rangers, Monks, anyone who wants to wear light armor and dodge a lot.  
  **Common checks**: Acrobatics (balance, tumbling), Sleight of Hand (pickpocketing, hiding objects), Stealth.

- **Constitution** (CON)  
  Health, stamina, toughness — how well your body resists poison, disease, exhaustion, and how much punishment you can take before dropping.  
  Adds directly to your Hit Points (HP) every level.  
  **Most important for**: Everyone (more HP = live longer), especially Barbarians and anyone in melee.  
  **Common checks**: Concentration on spells while taking damage, resisting poison/exhaustion.

- **Intelligence** (INT)  
  Memory, logic, education, reasoning — book smarts, puzzle solving, recalling lore.  
  **Most important for**: Wizards (their main spellcasting stat), Artificers, Investigators/Rogues who take Investigation skill.  
  **Common checks**: Arcana (magic knowledge), History, Investigation (searching for clues), Nature, Religion.

- **Wisdom** (WIS)  
  Perception, intuition, willpower, awareness of surroundings, common sense, insight into people’s motives.  
  **Most important for**: Clerics, Druids (main spellcasting stat), Rangers, Monks.  
  **Common checks**: Animal Handling, Insight (reading lies/motives), Medicine, Perception (noticing hidden things), Survival (tracking, foraging).

- **Charisma** (CHA)  
  Force of personality, confidence, charm, leadership, deception, intimidation, performance.  
  **Most important for**: Bards, Paladins, Sorcerers, Warlocks (main spellcasting stat), anyone who wants to talk their way out of (or into) trouble.  
  **Common checks**: Deception, Intimidation, Performance, Persuasion.

**Beginner tip**: Most new players start with one or two high scores (15–16+) in their class’s main stat(s), one decent Constitution (14+), and dump the rest where it hurts least. The sheet will show you live how each choice affects attacks, saves, skills, etc.

#### 3.2 Saving Throws (Below Ability Scores – Your Defenses Against Disaster)

**What Are Saving Throws? (The Absolute Beginner Explanation)**  
Saving throws (often just called "saves") are like **emergency dodge rolls** or **resistance checks**. They're what you roll when something **bad** is trying to hurt or control you—think fireballs exploding in your face, a dragon's poison breath, a wizard trying to charm you into their servant, or a trap that poisons you.

- **How a save works in play**:  
  1. The DM says: "Make a **Dexterity saving throw**!" (or CON, WIS, etc.)  
  2. You roll **1d20** (a 20-sided die).  
  3. Add your **ability modifier** (e.g., +3 DEX).  
  4. **If proficient**: Add your **Proficiency Bonus** (e.g., +2 at level 1, up to +6 at level 17).  
  5. **If expertise** (very rare): Add **double** proficiency.  
  6. Compare to the **DC** (Difficulty Class) the DM announces (usually 10–20+).  
     - **Success**: You dodge/resist! (Often half damage or no effect.)  
     - **Failure**: Full bad thing happens.  

**Example from a real game**:  
A goblin throws a *fireball* at your party. DM: "Dexterity save, DC 13!"  
- Your DEX mod is +2.  
- You're proficient (+2 prof bonus).  
- Roll: 14 + 2 + 2 = **18** → Success! You take 4d6 fire damage (half).  
- Your squishy wizard (no DEX prof) rolls 10 + 0 = 10 → Fail! Full 8d6 damage. Ouch.

**Why Save Proficiencies Matter So Much**:  
- Every class gets **2 free saves** (e.g., Barbarian: STR + CON). These are always proficient.  
- The sheet auto-adds them—no need to click anything.  
- Extra proficiencies come from:  
  - **Backgrounds** (e.g., Acolyte gives Insight + Religion, but not saves).  
  - **Feats** (e.g., Resilient: Pick any save and become proficient).  
  - **Class features** (e.g., Paladin's Aura of Protection adds CHA mod to *all* saves).  
- **Proficient saves = your "superpowers"**. They're what keep you alive against spells and traps.

1. **Scroll down** under Ability Scores—you'll see 6 rows, one per ability:  
   | Save Name       | Ability | Circle (Click Me!) | Total Bonus |

#### 3.4 Hit Points Section (Center – Big HP Box – Your Life Meter)

**What Are Hit Points? (The Complete Beginner Guide)**  
Hit Points (HP) are your character's **life total**—the number that tells you how much damage you can take before falling unconscious (or dying). Think of HP as your **"damage sponge"**: every sword swing, arrow, spell, or trap chip away at it. At 0 HP, you're down but not necessarily dead (that's where Death Saves come in).

- **HP isn't "realistic wounds"**—it's an abstraction. Losing 10 HP might mean a shallow cut, exhaustion, or a near-miss. Dropping to 0 = knocked out cold.
- **Why it matters**: Combat revolves around this. Deal more damage than the enemy's HP → they drop. Same for you.
- **Regaining HP**: Short rests (Hit Dice), long rests (+ all HP), potions, healing spells.

#### 8. Biography Tab – Your Character’s Soul & Story (The Roleplay & Personality Hub)

**What Is the Biography Tab? (The Heart of Your Hero)**  
This tab is **not about numbers** — it’s about **who your character is** as a person (or elf, dragonborn, tiefling, etc.). It’s the place to write their **backstory**, **appearance**, **personality quirks**, **ideals**, **bonds**, **flaws**, **allies**, **enemies**, and everything that makes them feel alive at the table.

In D&D, mechanics (HP, spells, attacks) keep you in the fight — **biography keeps you in the story**.  
This tab turns your sheet from a stat block into a **living character** that your Dungeon Master (DM) and fellow players can connect with.

**Tab Layout (Two-Column Grid)**

```
Left Column:                              Right Column:
├── Physical Description                  ├── Character History
│   - Age, Gender, Height, Weight         ├── Allies & Organizations
│   - Hair, Eyes, Skin, Appearance text   ├── Enemies
│                                           └── Character Summary (live preview card)
└── Personality
    - Traits, Ideals, Bonds, Flaws
```

**Step-by-Step: Bringing Your Character to Life**

**8.1 Physical Description (Left – Top Card)**  
Seven short fields + one big textarea:

| Field     | Example Input                     | Why It Matters at the Table                     |
|-----------|-----------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------|
| **Age**   | 127                               | Elves live centuries — age tells maturity/experience |
| **Gender**| Male / Female / Non-binary / etc.| Helps DM describe NPCs’ reactions               |
| **Height**| 5'10" (178 cm)                   | “You’re eye-level with the orc chief”           |
| **Weight**| 165 lb                            | “You’re wiry, not bulky”                        |
| **Hair**  | Silver with black streaks         | Memorable visual hook                           |
| **Eyes**  | Emerald green                     | “Your eyes glow faintly in the dark”            |
| **Skin**  | Deep bronze with faint scales     | Dragonborn heritage shines here                 |

**Appearance Description** (big box):  
- Free-write 3–10 sentences.  
- **Good examples**:  
  - “Tall and lean, with weathered leather armor patched from a dozen battles. A jagged scar runs across his left cheek. He carries himself with quiet confidence, always scanning for threats.”  
  - “A young tiefling woman with curling ram horns, crimson skin, and golden eyes that seem to catch every light. She wears flowing robes of deep indigo embroidered with silver arcane sigils.”

**Pro tip**: Keep it vivid but concise — DMs love stealing one striking detail for narration.

**8.2 Personality (Left – Bottom Card)**  
Four classic “background trait” boxes (from Player’s Handbook p. 125–141):

| Box       | What to Write                              | Example (Chaotic Good Rogue)                     |
|-----------|--------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------|
| **Personality Traits** | 1–2 short quirks or habits                | “I always have a joke ready, even in danger.”<br>“I collect interesting coins from every city I visit.” |
| **Ideals**     | Core belief or moral compass               | “Freedom. Chains are meant to be broken, whether literal or metaphorical.” |
| **Bonds**      | What ties you to the world/people          | “I owe my life to the thieves’ guild that took me in as an orphan.” |
| **Flaws**      | Real weaknesses or vices                   | “I can’t resist a shiny object — especially if it’s not nailed down.” |

- **Quick-select suggestions**: Some backgrounds offer pre-written options (click to auto-fill if you want inspiration).  
- **Why these matter**: DMs use them to tempt you (“A jeweled amulet — your flaw kicks in?”) or reward you (“Your bond to the guild gives you an informant here!”).

**8.3 Character History (Right – Top Card)**  
- **Big textarea**: Your full backstory.  
- **Recommended length**: 200–800 words.  
- **Good structure**:  
  1. Where/when born? Family?  
  2. Key life event(s) that shaped you?  
  3. Why adventuring now?  
  4. One secret or unanswered question?  
- **Example snippet**:  
  “Born in the slums of Waterdeep to a human mother and unknown elven father, Kael learned to survive by wits and quick fingers. At 16 he crossed the wrong noble — framed for theft, he fled the city with nothing but a dagger and a burning need to prove his innocence. Now he seeks powerful allies (or enemies) who can clear his name — or make the noble pay.”

**8.4 Allies & Organizations / Enemies (Right – Middle Cards)**  
- **Allies**: Guilds, mentors, old friends, patrons, family.  
  - “The Grey Hands of Waterdeep (loose contact).”  
  - “Old mentor Tharivol, retired elven ranger.”  
- **Enemies**: Rivals, nemeses, people hunting you.  
  - “Lord Varnholt — believes I stole his heirloom.”  
  - “The Red Hand orc tribe — I killed their war chief.”  
- **Use**: DMs love these — they weave your story into the campaign.

**8.5 Character Summary (Right – Bottom – Live Beautiful Preview)**  
- **Auto-generated card** (only shows once you fill basics):  
  ```

  Chaotic Good
  STR 10 (+0)  DEX 16 (+3)  CON 12 (+1)
  INT 13 (+1)  WIS 11 (+0)  CHA 14 (+2)
  ```
- **Stunning gradient background**, gold title — perfect screenshot for Discord or printing as a handout.

**Real-Play Biography Moments**  
- **Tavern scene**: “My character has emerald eyes and always smirks — he tries to charm the barmaid.”  
- **Moral dilemma**: “My flaw is greed — do I take the cursed ring?”  
- **Flashback**: “My bond to the guild means I recognize their tattoo on this stranger.”  
- **DM hook**: “Enemies include Lord Varnholt — he just walked into the throne room.”

**Pro Tips for Deep Roleplay**  
- **Keep flaws real** — “I’m a kleptomaniac” is funnier and more playable than “I’m evil.”  
- **One secret**: Give DM something to exploit later (e.g., “I’m actually a disguised noble”).  
- **Update over time**: Add new allies/enemies after sessions.  
- **Print this tab**: DM handout — “Here’s who my character is.”

**Common Newbie Traps & Fixes**

|------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------|
| Blank backstory                    | Start with 3 sentences: origin → inciting incident → goal |
| Overly edgy (“I’m a brooding loner”) | Add one positive trait + one funny flaw        |
| Ignoring bonds/flaws               | They’re DM gold — give them hooks!             |
| Forgetting to fill summary         | Once name/class filled → card auto-appears     |

**Live Sheet Magic**  
- **Name/class/alignment change** → Summary card updates instantly.  
- **Ability scores change** → Summary reflects new mods.  
- **Print-friendly**: Clean, readable — perfect character portrait page.

Biography tab = **your character’s beating heart**. Fill it with love, and your DM will love you back.
